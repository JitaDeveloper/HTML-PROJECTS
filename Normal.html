  • Introduction

    • Normal distribution often known as Gaussian distribution is a very much popular distribution among the statisticians.

    Normal distribution has the following properties-

    1. Bell shaped
    2. Symmetric
    3. Not a heavy tail

    Suppose the pdf of the distribution `f(x) ∝ e^(-ax^2), a>0;`

    So, `f(x)= ke^(-ax^2), k>0;`

    Now `int_RRe^(-ax^2)dx` = `2int_(0)^ooe^(-ax^2)dx`

    = `int_(0)^ooy^(1/2-1)e^(-ay)dy` = `sqrt(pi/a)`

    Let `y = x^2 `
    `dy=2xdx `
    `dx=1/2y^(-1/2)dy`

    Hence, `k = sqrt(a/pi)`
    `E(X) = kint_RRxe^(-ax^2)dx `

    ` = k( int_(-oo)^0xe^(-ax^2)+ int_(0)^ooxe^(-ax^2)dx)` `= 0;`

    as the integral
    converges to some gamma

    `E(X^2) = kint_RRx^2e^(-ax^2)dx` `= 2kint_(0)^oox^2e^(-ax^2)dx`; (assuming that the integral exists which is true;)
    ` = kint_(0)^ooy^(3/2-1)e^(-ay)dy` `= k(|~3/2)/a^(3/2) = k(1/2|~1/2)/a^(3/2)` `= k(1/2sqrt(pi))/a^(3/2)=1/(2a)`

    `σ^2=v(X) = E(X^2)-{E(X)}^2 = 1/(2a)` { as E(X)=0 }
    or, `a=1/(2sigma^2)`; So, `k = sqrt(1/(2pisigma^2)) = 1/(sigmasqrt(2pi))`
    Thus, we get `f(x)= 1/(sigmasqrt(2pi))e^(-x^2/(2sigma^2))`;

Define, `X^' = X + mu` where `muinRR`

`:. E(X^')=mu` as E(X)=0

PDF of X = `f(x)= 1/(sigmasqrt(2pi))e^(-x^2/(2sigma^2))` `x inRR` & `sigmainRR^+`

PDF of `X^'` = `g(x^')= 1/(sigmasqrt(2pi))e^(-(x^'-mu)^2/(2sigma^2))``x^' inRR`, `muinRR` & `sigmainRR^+`

g is the PDF of Gaussian Distribution with mean `mu` and variance `sigma^2` and is denoted by `N(mu,sigma^2)`.

Numerical values for density, cumulative density, fractiles are given in Biometrika vol. 1 for N(0,1) distribution.
